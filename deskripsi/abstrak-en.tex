\begin{abstract}

  Balance and stability are key aspects in the development of humanoid robots, especially when the robot has to perform activities involving foot movements with support on only one foot. This research aims to develop a balance system in humanoid robots using load cell sensors on the soles of the feet. The humanoid robot used in this research employs the VI-ROSE ITS robot with a total of 29 degrees of freedom (DoF). This system monitors the pressure changes on both soles of the robot and calculates the Center of Pressure (CoP) of the robot. The pressure center calculation uses four load cells on each sole processed by microcontrollers located on the soles and sent wirelessly to the main microcontroller. Sensor measurement errors range from 0-50 grams, with an average of 14.8 grams on the right foot and 14.6 grams on the left foot. The sensor can detect the center of pressure on the X-axis with a maximum value of 1.44 when lifting the right foot and a minimum of -1.33 when lifting the left foot. The robot can balance itself using PID control on 5 servos to adjust the roll position, with Kp = 0.10 and Kd = 0.005, achieving a 100\% success rate in maintaining balance when lifting a foot with a tilt of 3 degrees.


\end{abstract}
